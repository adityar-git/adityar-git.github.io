Visualization Of
Transcriptomic Data

Computational Drug Repositioning studies holds a promising future to predict new drug-phenotypes mapping of the drug with comparatively cheaper cost and lesser time. Additionally, With the NIH funded public data repository, LINCS transcriptomic data, which provides a comprehensive study for differential gene expression over 1.4 million probes for 12,000 genes, we are not at the technologically advanced phase to predict the various effect of drugs at a micro-level. In repurposing studies, various features of drugs hold predominant importance, although the ranking of such features to predict a particular phenotype still remains hazy. In this study, we aim to visualize and analyse various drugs in the LINCS L1000 dataset with their Chemical Fingerprints.

Drug Bank approved drug could be present in either of the five classes: Approved, Investigational, Neutraceutical, Illicit, Vet- Approved and Withdrawn .A drug may be present only in one category or maybe in more than one category. A drug with more than one category - say approved and investigational - would mean a drug has been approved for certain conditions on certain patients while is still under the process of approval for some others. We checked the information corresponding to all drugs in LINCS. The number of drugs in each category is as shown in the barplot. Clicking on the bar would give drugs corresponding to each category. We see more number of drugs in approved stage are taken in the experiment of LINCS.

Most drugs in the study are patented from the US and Canadian region of the globe. It is estimated that for the drug to be approved after the clinical-stage, around 2 million dollars are invested. The US and Canada have invested from 802 million dollars in 2003 to around 1.3 billion dollars in 2013 for the clinical study. Also to note here is the fact that a drug once approved can also be reverted back to the withdrawn stage after post-marketing surveillance, which leads to the serious financial impact on industry and health impact on people.

Many features of the drugs could be used to analyse the drugs and their mapping to various phenotypic conditions. One such vastly studied feature is Chemical structure of a drug. The 2D chemical structure of the drug is converted to MACCS chemical fingerprints, denoting the presence of 100 subunit in the chemical compound. Distance-level analysis of the chemical structure of the drug could tell us how closely the two drugs are related. For the hypothesis that similar drugs could be clustered together and may yield similar phenotype, we cluster 20,338 LINCS drug into cluster using hierarchical clustering. Clicking on the drug will give more information about the drug and the genes affected.
